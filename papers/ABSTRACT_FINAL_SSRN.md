# ABSTRACT FINAL - Paper SSRN

## "General Welfare and Common Good as Evolutionary Stable Strategies: An Extended Phenotype Analysis of Constitutional Emergency Language"

**Ignacio A. Lerer**  
*Working Paper - November 2025*

---

### ABSTRACT (150 palabras)

"General welfare" (*bienestar general*) and "common good" (*bien común*) appear synonymous in constitutional theory, yet in Argentine jurisprudence (1922-2025) they exhibit 17.8-fold fitness differential. Justice Rodolfo Barra's 1991 formulation "common good equals the good of the State" achieved zero fitness: no favorable citations and formal reversal within 15 years (*ALITT v. IGJ*, 2006). 

We apply Extended Phenotype Theory (Dawkins 1982) and Evolutionary Stable Strategies (Maynard Smith 1973) to explain this pattern. Vague constitutional language ("general welfare") enables mutation of temporary emergency powers into permanent State authority: Argentina's "provisional" income tax (1932) persists 93 years later. 

Cross-national analysis of 47 jurisdictions confirms: vague language increases mutation odds 17.6-fold (p<0.001), controlling for crisis severity. However, institutional friction (Switzerland's direct democracy, Canada's sunset clauses) prevents mutation despite vagueness. 

We derive five constitutional design principles for mutation-resistant emergency clauses. Fitness depends on context, not philosophical truth.

### ABSTRACT EXPANDIDO (250 palabras - versión opcional para SSRN)

**Research Question**: Why do some legal doctrines propagate successfully while others fail, even when formulated by apex courts?

**Case**: Argentine Supreme Court Justice Rodolfo Barra's 1991 doctrine "common good (*bien común*) equals the good of the State" in *Comunidad Homosexual Argentina v. IGJ* (denying legal status to LGBTQ+ organization). Despite originating from a 7-2 majority on the highest court, this doctrine achieved **zero reproductive fitness**: no favorable citations in 33 years (1991-2024), *de facto* reversal in 4 months (IGJ granted status March 1992), formal reversal in 15 years (*ALITT* 2006).

**Theoretical Framework**: We model constitutional doctrines as **replicating strategies** competing for fitness in variable institutional environments, using Evolutionary Game Theory (Maynard Smith 1982) and Extended Phenotype Theory (Dawkins 1982). The G-function (fitness function) calculates a doctrine's reproductive capacity based on environmental parameters: democracy score, international human rights pressure, clerical strength, judicial independence, social movement organization.

**Key Findings**:
1. **Predicted vs. empirical fitness**: Barra's doctrine had theoretical G-fitness of -0.636 in 1991 Argentina (democratizing context, rising LGBTQ+ movement, international pressure), predicting extinction. Empirical fitness = 0 citations confirmed prediction.

2. **Counterfactual validation**: Same doctrine would have had positive fitness (+0.55) during 1976-1983 dictatorship (Doctrine of National Security, State-centric ideology dominant), suggesting *context-dependence* not philosophical correctness determines success.

3. **Vague language enables mutation**: Comparative analysis of 47 jurisdictions shows vague constitutional terms ("general welfare," "public order," "security") increase probability of permanent emergency powers 17.6× (odds ratio, p<0.001). Argentina's "provisional" income tax (1932) exemplifies: 93 years later, no sunset.

4. **Institutional friction prevents mutation**: Switzerland (10 emergency invocations in 110 years, all terminated) and Canada (2 invocations in 37 years, both brief) resist mutation through: mandatory sunset clauses, direct democracy referendums, active judicial review, federal-provincial friction. Same vague language, opposite outcomes.

**Theoretical Contribution**: Dissolves essentialist debates (liberalism vs. communitarianism, originalism vs. living constitutionalism) into empirical question: which strategy has fitness in *this* environment? Success depends on **genealogical plausibility** (continuity with established traditions), **carrier networks** (institutional actors propagating doctrine), and **environmental compatibility** (alignment with political regime, social movements, international norms).

**Practical Implications**: For constitutional designers, vague language is unavoidable (emergencies unpredictable by nature) but manageable through **multiplying institutional checks**: automatic sunsets, mandatory judicial review, legislative oversight, federal-provincial balance, ex post accountability (public inquiries). For litigants/academics, **timing is critical**: even philosophically sound arguments fail if environment hostile (Barra's "anacronismo ideológico"—authoritarian discourse in democratic context).

**Methodological Innovation**: First systematic study of **failed doctrinal mutations** in constitutional law. Existing literature focuses on successful precedents; we analyze the "evolutionary cemetery" of extinct doctrines, revealing selection pressures.
